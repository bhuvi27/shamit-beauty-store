"You've just joined as a Staff ML Engineer at ICICI Lombard, India's largest private general insurance company. Your CTO pulls you into a strategy meeting on your first week.

Here's the situation:

ICICI Lombard processes ~45,000 motor insurance claims per month. Today, a typical claim takes 12–18 days from filing to settlement. The industry target — set by IRDAI (India's insurance regulator) — is 72 hours for straightforward claims. Your competitors (Bajaj Allianz, HDFC ERGO) are already marketing 'instant settlement' for simple cases, even if the reality is messier than the marketing.

Here's what the current process looks like:

A policyholder gets into a fender-bender in Bangalore traffic. They call the helpline or open the app. Over the next 2–3 days, a series of manual steps unfold:

Photo submission: The customer uploads 6–15 photos of the damage from their phone — varying quality, angles, lighting. Some are blurry. Some show the wrong car. Some include photos of the other vehicle, the road, even selfies.

Document submission: The customer uploads their policy document, driving license, FIR copy (if applicable), and repair estimates from a garage. These come in as a mix of PDFs, photos of paper documents, and WhatsApp-forwarded images of scanned documents. Some are rotated, some are cropped, some are in Hindi or Kannada.

Surveyor assignment: A physical surveyor is assigned. They visit the garage (sometimes 3–5 days later), inspect the vehicle, take their own photos, and write a survey report estimating repair costs. This is the biggest bottleneck — ICICI has ~2,000 surveyors covering all of India.

Adjudication: A claims adjudicator (human) reviews everything — the customer's photos, the surveyor's report, the policy terms, the repair estimate — and makes a decision: approve, partially approve, or reject. They check coverage limits, deductibles, exclusions, whether the damage is consistent with the reported incident, and whether the repair costs are reasonable for the damage shown.

Fraud check: A separate fraud team reviews ~15% of claims flagged by manual rules — recycled photos from previous claims, inflated repair estimates, staged accidents, claims filed within days of policy purchase.

Your CTO's ask:

'I want an AI-powered claims agent that can handle 60% of straightforward motor claims end-to-end without a human surveyor or adjudicator, reducing settlement time from 12 days to under 3 hours for those cases. The remaining 40% (complex, high-value, or suspicious claims) should still go to humans, but with AI-generated summaries and recommendations that cut their review time in half.

We have:

3 years of historical claims data: ~1.6 million resolved claims with photos, documents, survey reports, adjudication decisions, and fraud labels

Policy documents for all active policies (~12 million)

A garage network database with standard repair cost benchmarks by vehicle model, part, and city

An existing OCR pipeline that works decently on English documents but struggles with regional languages and handwritten content
